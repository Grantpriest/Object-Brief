Object Website Brief

Due 24 June (TBC)

Overview

Choose an object which intrigues you, which you feel resonates with you. Use your intuition. There are no bad choices. Ideally it's an object you have in your possesion.

Think about the materiality of your object, how it looks, how it feels, how it integrates into their daily lives, what memories and associations it conjures. Is there a kind of poetry or beauty in its design? Has it been made with care? What effect might it have on the environment? What materials is it made from? What are its dimensions? How much does it weigh? What does it feel like? How would you describe its function to someone from another planet?

This project initiates a semester long process in which you will begin to establish the basis of an individual design practice. This includes exploring methodologies of research, visual experimentation and self-directed learning.

Required Outputs

Website

Create a website using HTML and CSS (Javascript optional) which documents your findings and visual explorations of your chosen object.

The website itself should be considered a 'lab', a place for exploring ways of representing the object, it's history, it's material qualities, its contemporary context, how it's portrayed in popular media etc.

It should also be a space to explore how a website lends itself to making connections or 'linking' things together. What techniques can you find for showing connections between things. What can you do online that you can't do in print? Consider utilising moving image, sound, photography, film, animation etc.

Do not feel the need to portray your expertise regarding your object, it's history, etc. Its more important to act than to know, sometimes mis-understanding something can lead to more interesting connections.

Host your website on www.github.com
(Follow the instructions here: https://pages.github.com/ )

Poster

Develop a poster (or series of posters) in conjunction with the development of your website, these two outcomes should complement each other and be developed in tandem.

This is a good place to begin quickly developing visual ideas and sketches before integrating them into your website.

Assessment Procedure


Your Semester One Studio Projects will be assessed at the end of semester (TBC June 15—26). You will submit your final design work, development and edited research in an allotted space. Your mid-year submission will be worth 50% of your final grade. You will receive feedback and final letter grade for all Semester One Studio work. You will not receive individual project grades.
